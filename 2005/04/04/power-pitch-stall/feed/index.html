]]> By: david https://lahso.megginson.com/2005/04/04/power-pitch-stall/#comment-49 Sun, 04 Dec 2005 15:48:20 +0000 /?p=56#comment-49 Thanks for the comment, Joel. Memorizing a standard pitch angle for a specific airspeed at a specific power setting works only when weight and density altitude are roughly constant. Unfortunately, while they tend to be (close to) constant during training, one of the first flights a person takes after a PPL typically involves loading up the plane with buddies or family, and suddenly the old, memorized pitch angles become very dangerous, and we start to see those fatal departure-stall accidents (especially in the summer). You’ll still have to use pitch angle in any given flight to avoid chasing the ASI, but you cannot safely use a pitch angle that you’ve memorized in advance — you need to establish a new pitch angle every time you fly.
