I actually am not quite sure on why the inputs are averaged, though I’m sure a quick google search would yield a lot of answers;
But I do know that (I’m not a professional pilot, just a flightsimmer with too much time virtually in the A320 series)

Airbus has a “Sidestick Priority” feature. It’s a small button on both joysticks. Pressing it deactivates the autopilot, and gives control priority to whoever pushes the button (So the plane disregards input from the other side stick.) If the button is pressed for more than 30 seconds, it becomes latched, and the stick will keep priority even if the button is released.
Pressing either button again disconnects the priority in this case.

To ensure both pilots know who has priority, both of them have a small annunciator light with an arrow pointing to the other side of the cockpit, together with a voice warning.
http://www.airliners.net/photo/319771/L/ Top right shows a good example of the side stick priority.

Hope it helps atleast a little!
